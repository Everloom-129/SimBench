Every benchmark in this collection is a proxy for the real world. This capstone places them on a single reality ladder — from pristine simulators (where VLAs hit 95%+) through perturbed sim and real-to-sim, down to physical robot arms, where the best models barely clear 50%. It then details the two frontier real-evaluation efforts: RoboChallenge (online real-robot leaderboard) and ManipArena (Real2Sim paired environments). The throughline of the whole survey: the more real the test, the lower the score.

Sim is a forecast; real is the outcome. Pure-sim leaderboards (LIBERO, RLBench, CALVIN) are saturated and optimistic. Perturbation suites (LIBERO-Plus, COLOSSEUM) and real-to-sim suites (SimplerEnv) exist to make sim predict real — COLOSSEUM reports a sim↔real correlation of R²≈0.61. But the ground truth is a physical arm, and there the numbers are humbling: RoboChallenge's leader sits at 50%, ManipArena's best model clears 9/10 on no more than two of its tasks.
