LIBERO-PRO asks whether VLAs understand their tasks or merely memorize trajectories. It re-runs LIBERO under reasonable perturbations along four axes — manipulated objects, initial position, task instructions, and environment — that a competent policy should shrug off. The result is damning: models at 90%+ on standard LIBERO drop to 0.0% the moment an object’s position changes, and they execute the old motion even when the instruction is replaced with a different task — evidence they read coordinates, not scenes, and ignore language.

Two perturbations expose memorization. Position (move the object inside its valid region) and Task (give a genuinely different instruction) drive almost every model to 0% — the policy reproduces a memorized trajectory regardless of where the object is or what was asked. By contrast Object (attribute swaps) and Semantic (paraphrase) are nearly free, because they don’t change the rote motion. π0.5 is the only model with any Position robustness.
