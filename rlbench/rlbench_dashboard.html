RLBench is the classic large-scale manipulation benchmark: 106 hand-designed task classes on a 7-DoF Franka with four RGB-D cameras, each carrying multiple variations and template language. Most policies are keypose learners (predict next end-effector pose, not dense actions). The community converged on an 18-task multi-task split — this atlas catalogs all 106 tasks and renders the canonical per-task leaderboard across nine methods, from Image-BC to RVT-2.

The 18-task benchmark. PerAct / RVT / RVT-2 train one multi-task policy on 18 tasks from 100 demos each, evaluated over 25 episodes × multiple seeds. Cells below are success %. Tasks like Insert Peg, Sort Shape and Stack Cups are high-precision and stay hard even for the best policy; Push Buttons and Meat off Grill saturate. RLBench reports no single "language instruction" per task — each variation samples a template — so this atlas shows the task taxonomy and success matrix rather than an instruction pool.
