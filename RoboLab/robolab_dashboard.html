RoboLab is NVIDIA SRL's high-fidelity Isaac-Sim manipulation benchmark built for the analysis of task-generalist policies. Rather than report one success number, it unfolds RoboLab-120's 120 tabletop tasks along three axes: every task ships three language-specificity tiers (vague / default / specific), three difficulty labels (simple / moderate / complex), and per-task attribute tags rolled up into three competency axes — visual / procedural / relational. The leaderboard scores 120 tasks × 10 episodes = 1,200 evaluations on SR%, a subtask-progress Score, and end-effector motion quality (EE Speed / EE SPARC) — and its policy ranking correlates strongly with real-world RoboArena Elo.

What this benchmark really answers. RoboLab turns "one number" into a diagnostic grid: the same task is evaluated under vague → default → specific phrasings, difficulty is explicitly labeled, and attribute tags (color, spatial, counting, stacking…) roll up into three competency axes. A failure is no longer just "failure" — it localizes to not seeing (visual), not acting (procedural), or not reasoning over multi-object relations (relational).
