LIBERO is the most-reported VLA capability benchmark: 4 evaluation suites of 10 tasks each, plus a 90-task procedural library, all on a Franka arm with templated language. Its design isolates distribution shifts one at a time — Spatial, Object, Goal, and long-horizon Long. This atlas catalogs all 131 task instructions and renders the leaderboard — whose real message is that standard LIBERO is now saturated (97%+), with the Long suite the last holdout.

Saturated — read with care. On Spatial/Object/Goal, the strongest policies (OpenVLA-OFT, π0) now exceed 96%; the benchmark no longer separates them there. Only the Long suite still has headroom. The survey's caution: a 97% LIBERO number says little about robustness — the same models can collapse to single digits under a viewpoint or position shift (see LIBERO-Plus / LIBERO-PRO).
