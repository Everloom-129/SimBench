Not a benchmark. A correlation pipeline.

PolaRiS · Scalable
Real-to-Sim Evaluation

PolaRiS turns a 2–5 minute phone scan of a real scene into a Gaussian-splat simulation you can evaluate policies in. The headline isn't task count — it's fidelity: sim scores that track real-world performance (worst-case per-env r=0.81, mean r≈0.9, r=0.98 vs RoboArena). The 6 environments here aren't a coverage suite; they're the validation sample proving the correlation holds.

Why only 6 tasks? Because the count is a validation budget, not a product. To compute real-sim correlation you need paired real-robot ground truth (20 rollouts each), which is expensive, slow, and non-reproducible — the very problem PolaRiS exists to solve. So 6 is the ceiling of affordable paired real eval across two institutions (UW + Princeton), not a limit on the simulator. Scale is meant to emerge from the pipeline (<20 min/env), zero-shot co-training (15 co-train envs, disjoint from the 6 eval envs), and a community Hub — "like it is commonplace for LLM research today." Section 04 places this against the coverage benchmarks.

02

Why correlation beats coverage

the LIBERO-90 counter-argument, visualized
PolaRiS's own argument against coverage: LIBERO-90 runs 90 tasks × 50 rollouts × 50k fine-tune steps (~10× the compute), yet nearly every policy lands in the 90–95% band — indistinguishable, weak correlation. 6 high-correlation tasks (r≈0.9) separate strong from weak policies that 90 saturated tasks cannot. Signal quality > task count.
03

The 6 validation environments

rubric-scored, rigid-body reach→lift→place
Honest limit: all 6 are rigid-body reach → lift → place (Pan Clean is the only near-exception, a scrub). The authors explicitly exclude non-rigid, soft-body, and complex-contact skills — "only capture a small subset of the capabilities tested in RoboArena." So PolaRiS's diversity claim is about visual-scene variety (two institutions, varied tabletops/lighting/backgrounds), not action-semantic variety. That's the right diversity axis for a real-to-sim tool, since correlation depends on visual domain gap — but it is not task-skill coverage.
04

Coverage vs. correlation

why these 6 benchmarks' task counts are not comparable
BenchmarkType#TasksWhat the task count actually meansSimulatorCorrelation metric
Coverage — task count = the product (breadth is the deliverable) Correlation — task count = validation budget (signal is the deliverable)
The takeaway for competitive analysis: PolaRiS belongs with SimplerEnv (real-to-sim correlation tools), not with RoboCasa / RoboTwin / BEHAVIOR (task-coverage benchmarks). Comparing their task counts is a category error — for coverage benchmarks the count is the product; for correlation tools it's a validation budget bounded by how much paired real-robot eval you can afford. A correlation tool with 6 well-chosen tasks and r≈0.9 is doing something a 365-task coverage suite is not even trying to do.
